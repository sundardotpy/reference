Why Coupon Alone Is Not Enough

An FD is simple — you put in money, earn the stated rate, get it back. Bonds work differently because they are bought and sold in the market at varying prices.

SituationYou PayYou Receive at MaturityImpact
Bought at Discount₹950₹1,000Extra ₹50 gain
Bought at Par₹1,000₹1,000No gain or loss
Bought at Premium₹1,050₹1,000₹50 loss

The coupon rate does not capture this gain or loss. Only YTM does. That is why we show YTM — it is the complete, honest picture.

Use the FD Analogy

"When you invest in an FD, the bank tells you one simple number — say 7% per year. You put in money, it grows at 7%, and you get it back at the end. Simple, right?

Now, bonds also pay you interest — we call it the Coupon Rate. For example, a bond might say 8% coupon, which means if the bond's face value is ₹1,000, you will receive ₹80 every year as interest. So far, sounds just like an FD. But here is where bonds are a little different..."
Step 2 — Introduce the Price Difference

Why the Price You Pay Matters

"Unlike an FD where you always put in exactly the amount the bank asks, bonds are bought and sold in the market — just like shares. So the price you actually pay for a bond may not be exactly ₹1,000. But no matter what you paid, the company will always pay you back exactly ₹1,000 at maturity.

So if you paid only ₹950, you will get ₹1,000 back — that extra ₹50 is also your profit! And if you paid ₹1,050, you will only get ₹1,000 back — meaning a small loss of ₹50. The coupon rate does not show this. Only YTM does."
Step 3 — Define YTM Simply

What YTM Includes

YTM — Yield to Maturity — is the true, all-in return you earn if you hold the bond until its end date.

Why Does the Bond Price Change on Our Platform?

Three key reasons to explain to customers when they notice price movements

Reason 1 — Accrued Interest

The Price Includes Interest Already Earned

When a bond is bought between two coupon payment dates, the price shown on the platform includes the interest that has been accumulating since the last coupon was paid. This is called accrued interest.

"Think of it like this — if you are buying someone's FD mid-way, you are also compensating them for the interest they have already earned up to that day. So the price you see on the platform on any given day reflects the bond's clean price plus this accrued interest. It is not a hidden charge — it is interest that rightfully belongs to the previous holder, and you will recover it when the next coupon is paid to you in full."
Reason 2 — Special Offers & Zero Brokerage Markup

You May Earn Extra YTM Through Our Offers

From time to time, our platform announces special offers where we do not charge our standard brokerage markup of 0.25%. During these offer periods, the effective YTM for the customer is higher because the price they pay is lower.

"We periodically run offers where we waive our brokerage markup of 0.25%. This directly benefits you — since you are paying a slightly lower price for the same bond, your YTM effectively goes up. So if you invest during an offer period, the return you see on your platform will be better than usual. We recommend keeping an eye on our app notifications so you do not miss these."
Reason 3 — Bond SIP Option

Get an Effective Extra 0.5% YTM with Bond SIP

When a customer chooses the Bond SIP option on our platform, they benefit from an additional effective YTM boost of approximately 0.5%. This is because the SIP structure allows us to pass on further cost benefits to the investor.

"If you invest through our Bond SIP option, you get an effective extra 0.5% YTM compared to a one-time lump sum investment in the same bond. "The exact calculation uses financial mathematics, but here is the simple idea:

YTM is the one single interest rate that, when applied to all your future cash flows — the coupon payments and the final maturity amount — gives you back exactly the price you paid today.

In plain words: What you pay today = Present value of all future coupons + Present value of maturity amount, discounted at YTM.

Our platform calculates this automatically for every bond, so you never have to do this math yourself. A bond with YTM of 8.5% is broadly equivalent to an FD giving you 8.5% — that is the simplest way to think about it."
