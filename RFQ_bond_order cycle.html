RFQ Bond Order Journey on Wint Wealth

Important: Payment success does not mean the investment is complete. The order is complete only after RFQ execution, NCL settlement, and bond credit to demat.
Key Participants
Investor
Places bond order and pays from registered bank account. Receives bond units in demat after settlement.
Cashfree
Payment gateway. Collects payment, confirms status, and performs TPV (Third Party Verification) check.
Wint Wealth
Broker / OBPP.
NSE RFQ
Exchange platform where the bond deal terms are captured and executed.
NCL
NSE Clearing Ltd. Handles final settlement of funds and securities.
NSDL Depository
Depository holds demat of investors, where units settled once order completed.

12-Step Success Journey
1
Investor Selects Bond / SIP Investor + Wint Wealth
Investor chooses bond or SIP on Wint platform. Wint verifies basic eligibility — KYC, verified bank account, demat availability, bond availability, and order limits.
Initiation
2
Payment Initiated through Cashfree Cashfree
Investor is directed to Cashfree payment page. Payment is attempted via UPI, net banking, mandate debit, or other enabled modes from the registered bank account.
Payment
3
Authentication & TPV Check Cashfree + Bank
Bank authenticates the payment (OTP / UPI approval). Cashfree performs Third Party Verification (TPV) — checks whether the paying bank account matches the KYC-registered bank account. This is mandatory for OBPP/SEBI-compliance flows.
ValidationTPV
4
Payment Success Confirmed Cashfree
Cashfree confirms payment success along with TPV/bank validation result and payment reference details. Money is now collected. However, the bond order is NOT yet complete at this point.
Payment
5
Wint Processes the Order Wint Wealth
Wint processes the order and routes it to NSE RFQ. Depending on the order type and investor profile, applicable backend checks may run at this stage. If any required check is not cleared, the order will not be routed to the exchange.
Order Processing Wint Validation
6
Order Routed to NSE RFQ Wint → NSE RFQ
Once all checks pass, Wint routes the bond order to the NSE RFQ (Request for Quote) platform. Here, the buy/sell quote, yield, price, quantity, and deal terms are captured. NSE maintains a full audit trail.
Exchange
7
Trade Executed on RFQ NSE RFQ
Once the quote is accepted and matched, the RFQ trade is considered executed. Price, yield, and quantity are finalised. The order has now moved from "payment collected" to "trade executed" — but settlement is still pending.
Trade Executed
8
NCL Receives Settlement Obligations NCL
After trade execution, the obligation moves to NSE Clearing Ltd. (NCL). Corporate bond trades reported on RFQ are eligible for settlement through NCL on a DVP I (Delivery vs Payment) basis — gross settlement for both securities and funds.
Settlement
9
Funds & Securities Pay-in Completed NCL
Both sides complete pay-in within the cut-off time. Buyer side: funds are made available to NCL. Seller side: bond units are transferred to NCL-specified depository account. Only when both sides meet obligations does NCL proceed.
Pay-in
10
NCL Payout Completed NCL
NCL completes payout: buyer receives bond units, seller receives funds. NCL transfers securities/funds to the counterparty's depository/bank account after successful pay-in from both sides.
Payout
11
Bond Units Credited to Demat Depository / DP
After NCL settlement, bond units are credited to the investor's demat account by the Depository Participant. This is the final stage of the investment journey.
Demat Credit
12
Order Marked Completed Wint Wealth
The bond order is now fully complete. Bond units are visible in the investor's demat account. Wint updates the order status to Completed.
Order Complete
Major Failures
A: Payment Gateway Failure
Before Order Placement
OTP failed or expired during authentication
UPI approval failed or timed out
Bank page timeout or bank declined payment
Payment status stuck as Pending
Outcome:
• Order does not move to RFQ
• If amount not debited → investor can retry directly
• If amount debited but failed → auto reversal within 3–5 working days as per bank timeline
• If pending → bank takes 2–3 working days to share final status
B: TPV / eg. Non-Registered Bank Failure
Before Valid Order
Payment made from a bank account not registered on the platform
Bank account mismatch detected during TPV
Cashfree "Payer Account Validation Failure" — account details do not match
Outcome:
• Payment cannot be used for investment
• Order is not placed on RFQ
• Refund/reversal initiated to the source bank account
• Investor must retry using registered bank account only
• Refund within 5–7 business days as per SOP
C: Order Processing Failure due to KYC
After Payment Success
UCC not created or missing within T+3 days
KRA not completed within required timeline
AML check not successfully completed
Demat account or KYC issue
Outcome:
• Order is not placed on RFQ
• Order marked REFUND_INITIATED after T+4 (after 2 PM)
• NCL refund flow initiated (5–7 working days)
• UCC cleanup required (T+5 working days)
• Investor can retry only after cleanup is completed

Two Types of Refund — Important Distinction
Payment Gateway Refund
Triggered when payment/authentication/TPV fails — before a valid order is placed.
Authentication failed (OTP, UPI, bank timeout)
Non-registered bank account used
TPV validation failure
Bank technical issue
Handled by  Cashfree / Bank refund flow
UPI refund: ~5 days  |  Card refund: ~7 days (after initiation)

KYC issue detected after payment

Important
Always identify the type of refund before giving timeline. Payment gateway refund (Cashfree/bank level) has a different source and timeline compared to the NCL/order refund. Do not quote the same timeline for both. If in doubt, check whether failed in during payment (gateway refund) or KYC/UCC/KRA failed (NCL refund).
