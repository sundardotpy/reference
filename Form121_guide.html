FORM 121

Declaration under Section 393(6) for receipt of interest income without TDS deduction. Use this guide to help customers fill each field correctly for Tax Year 2026–2027.

Part A Declarant details (Fields 1–8)
1
Full Name RequiredAs per PAN card
▼
What to enter
Full legal name exactly as it appears on PAN card no nicknames or short forms
Common mistake
Customer uses a different spelling or initials. This must match PAN records precisely.
"Please check the name printed on your PAN card and enter it exactly the same way even a small difference in spelling can cause issues with the declaration."
eg
Example
Write: RAMESH KUMAR SHARMA exactly as printed on PAN. Not "Ramesh Sharma" or "R.K. Sharma".
2
Complete Address RequiredCurrent residential address with PIN code
▼
What to enter
House/flat number, street name, area, city, state, and 6-digit PIN code
Common mistake
Customer skips the PIN code or gives an incomplete address both are needed
"Please write your full current address including your 6-digit PIN code this is needed for official communication related to your declaration."
eg
Example
Write: Flat 4B, Sunshine Apartments, MG Road, Koramangala, Bengaluru, Karnataka – 560034. PIN code 560034 is mandatory at the end.
3
PAN Number Required10-character Permanent Account Number
▼
What to enter
10-character alphanumeric PAN format: ABCDE1234F (5 letters + 4 digits + 1 letter)
Common mistake
Typos in PAN, entering Aadhaar number instead of PAN, or using lowercase letters
"Your PAN is a 10-character code 5 letters, then 4 numbers, then 1 letter again. You can find it on your PAN card or income tax e-filing account."
Important note
Without a valid PAN, TDS deduction cannot be stopped. This field is critical.
eg
Example
Write: ABCRS1234K 5 letters (ABCRS) + 4 digits (1234) + 1 letter (K). Always in capital letters. Do not enter Aadhaar number here.
4
Status RequiredINDIVIDUAL or HUF
▼
What to enter
Write "INDIVIDUAL" for a single person, "HUF" for Hindu Undivided Family
Common mistake
Customers often leave this blank or write "salaried" guide them to use only these two options
"For most customers, the status will be INDIVIDUAL. Only enter HUF if this declaration is for a Hindu Undivided Family account."
eg
Example
Ramesh is filing for himself, so write: INDIVIDUAL. If it were a joint family account, it would be HUF.
5
Residential Status RequiredResident or Non-Resident
▼
What to enter
Write "Resident" this form is only applicable to Indian residents
Common mistake
NRIs sometimes try to use this form they are not eligible for Form 121
"This form is only for resident Indians. If your customer is an NRI, kindly inform them that Form 121 does not apply to them for TDS exemption."
eg
Example
Ramesh lives and works in Bengaluru, so write: Resident.
5a
Age 60 years or more? ConditionalOnly for resident individuals YES or NO
▼
What to enter
Write YES if the customer is 60 years or older at any point during tax year 2026–27. Otherwise write NO.
Why this matters
Senior citizens (60+) have a higher basic exemption limit the income threshold for nil tax is higher for them
"If you are 60 years of age or above during this financial year (April 2026 to March 2027), please write YES. This affects your income exemption limit."
eg
Example
Ramesh is 45 years old, so write: NO. His exemption limit is ₹2,50,000. If he were 63 years old, it would be YES and his limit would be ₹3,00,000.
6
Email ID RequiredValid email for official communication
▼
What to enter
A working email address that the customer regularly checks
Common mistake
Typing errors in email missing "@", wrong domain (like .cm instead of .com)
"Please enter the email address where you receive your account statements this is where any official tax-related communication will be sent."
eg
Example
Write: ramesh.sharma@gmail.com must have "@" and a valid domain like .com, .in, .co.in. Double-check spelling before submitting.
7
Contact Number RequiredCountry code +91 and 10-digit mobile number
▼
What to enter
Country code is already +91 (India). Enter 10-digit mobile number in the "Number" field.
Common mistake
Customer enters landline number, or adds "0" before the mobile number only 10 digits needed
"The country code +91 is already filled in for India. Please just enter your 10-digit mobile number in the number field, without adding 0 at the beginning."
eg
Example
Country Code: +91  |  Number: 9876543210. Do not write "09876543210" the leading 0 is not needed.
8
Tax Year RequiredPre-filled as 2026–2027
▼
What to enter
Write "2026–2027" this is the current applicable tax year
Common mistake
Some customers confuse Assessment Year with Tax Year guide them to use 2026–2027
"The tax year for this declaration is 2026–2027 this means it covers income earned between April 2026 and March 2027. Please write exactly '2026–2027' in this field."
eg
Example
Write: 2026–2027. This covers the period 1st April 2026 to 31st March 2027.
Part A Income details (Fields 9–13)
9
Nature of Income RequiredPre-set as "Interest on Securities"
▼
What to enter
Write "Interest on Securities" this is fixed for bond investments
Common mistake
Customer writes "FD interest" or "bond income" must specifically say "Interest on Securities"
"In this field, please write 'Interest on Securities' this is the standard description for income from bonds, and using the exact wording is important for the declaration to be valid."
eg
Example
Write exactly: Interest on Securities. Not "Bond Interest", not "FD Interest" the exact phrase is mandatory.
10
Estimated Income (No TDS) RequiredAmount from our platform on which TDS should not be cut
▼
What to enter
The expected interest income from bonds held on our platform for the full tax year 2026–27
Common mistake
Customer enters previous year's income, or total portfolio value instead of expected interest income
"Please enter the total interest income you expect to receive from your bonds on our platform between April 2026 and March 2027. You can find the estimated payout in your account dashboard."
Important note
This is the amount from our platform only. If the customer holds bonds elsewhere, those are covered by separate Form 121 submissions (see Field 11).
eg
Example
Ramesh holds bonds worth ₹18 lakh on our platform at 10% annual interest. His estimated interest from our platform = ₹1,80,000. He enters this amount here.
11
Other Form 121 Filed Elsewhere OptionalOnly if submitted at another institution too
▼
What to enter
Write YES if this customer has already submitted Form 121 at another broker or bank. Write NO if this is the only one.
Sub-fields
11(a): Number of other Form 121 submitted. 11(b): Total amount declared in those other forms.
"Have you submitted a similar Form 121 at any other bank or broker for this financial year? If yes, please mention how many forms and the total income declared in those forms."
eg
Example
Ramesh also holds bonds at HDFC Bank and submitted Form 121 there. So: Field 11 = YES  |  11(a) = 1 (one other form filed)  |  11(b) = ₹40,000 (interest declared at HDFC Bank).
12
Aggregate Declared Income RequiredField 10 + Field 11(b) combined total
▼
What to enter
Simple addition: amount in Field 10 + amount in Field 11(b). If no other forms filed, Field 12 = Field 10.
Common mistake
Customer leaves this blank thinking it is auto-calculated it needs to be filled manually
"This field is just a total please add the amount from Field 10 and Field 11(b) and write the sum here. If you haven't filed Form 121 anywhere else, this will be the same as Field 10."
eg
Example
Ramesh's Field 10 = ₹1,80,000 + Field 11(b) = ₹40,000. So Field 12 = ₹2,20,000. This is the total bond interest he is declaring across all platforms this year.
13
Total Annual Income (All Sources) RequiredMost critical field must be below taxable limit
▼
What to enter
Customer's total estimated income for the year from ALL sources salary, rent, interest, business, etc.
Eligibility check
Below 60 yrs: under ₹2.5 lakh. Age 60–80 yrs: under ₹3 lakh. Age 80+: under ₹5 lakh.
"Please mention your total expected income from all sources this financial year salary, pension, rent, interest from all investments. This total must be below the basic exemption limit for this form to be valid."
Critical warning
If income exceeds the exemption limit, the customer is NOT eligible for Form 121. Filing it incorrectly can result in penalty or prosecution. Always verify this with the customer.
eg
Example
Ramesh's only income is bond interest ₹2,20,000 total (from Field 12). He has no salary or other income. So Field 13 = ₹2,20,000. This is below the ₹2,50,000 limit for his age (45 yrs), so he is eligible ✓.

ITR Details (Last 2 Years) OptionalAcknowledgment number and return income if ITR was filed
▼
What to enter
Tax Year (e.g. 2024–25), Acknowledgment Number from ITR filed, and the income declared in that return
Common mistake
Customer doesn't have acknowledgment number handy ask them to check the IT e-filing portal or their CA
"If you have filed income tax returns in the past two years, please share the acknowledgment number and the income amount declared. You can find this on the IT e-filing portal at incometax.gov.in under 'View Filed Returns'."
eg
Example
Row 1: Tax Year 2024–25  |  Ack No. 123456789012345  |  Return Income ₹2,10,000. Row 2: Tax Year 2023–24  |  Ack No. 987654321098765  |  Return Income ₹1,95,000.
Declaration section
✓
Signature + Place + Date RequiredCustomer's signature with place and date of signing
▼
What to enter
Physical or digital signature, city name in "Place", and today's date in DD/MM/YYYY format
Common mistake
Customer leaves Place or Date blank unsigned or undated declarations are not valid
"Please sign the declaration at the bottom and also fill in your city name and today's date. Without the signature and date, this form is not considered valid."
Legal note
The declaration confirms that the customer's total tax liability is NIL for the year. A false declaration can result in prosecution under the Income Tax Act. Remind customers to be truthful.
eg
Example
Place: Bengaluru  |  Date: 18/04/2026  |  Followed by Ramesh's signature and printed name: RAMESH KUMAR SHARMA.

✓ Quick eligibility checklist for agents

✓Customer is a Resident Indian (not NRI)
✓Total annual income is below the basic exemption limit (₹2.5L / ₹3L / ₹5L based on age)
✓PAN is valid and active
✓Income is from "Interest on Securities" bond interest only
✓If forms filed elsewhere, total aggregate declared income is still within the nil-tax limit
